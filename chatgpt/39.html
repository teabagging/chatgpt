John started weightlifting when he was 16. When he first started he could Clean & Jerk 80 kg and he could Snatch some weight. He managed to double his clean and jerk and increase his snatch by 80%. His new combined total lifting capacity is 250 kg. What was his initial Snatch weight?

We need to find his *initial* Snatch weight.2. **Identify the Unknown:** The main unknown is John's initial Snatch weight. Let's represent this with a variable. Let's use 'S' for the initial Snatch weight.3. **Break Down the Information:** Read the problem sentence by sentence and convert the words into mathematical expressions. * "John started weightlifting when he was 16." This is background info, not directly relevant to the calculations. * "When he first started he could Clean & Jerk 80 kg..." Initial C&J = 80 kg. * "...and he could Snatch some weight." We already defined this as 'S'. * "He managed to double his clean and jerk..." New C&J = 2 * (Initial C&J) = 2 * 80 kg = 160 kg. * "...and increase his snatch by 80%." Increase in Snatch = 80% of S = 0.80 * S. New Snatch = Initial Snatch + Increase in Snatch = S + 0.80 * S = 1.80 * S. * "His new combined total lifting capacity is 250 kg." New Combined Total = New C&J + New Snatch = 250 kg.4. **Formulate an Equation:** We can now write an equation using the information we've extracted and the unknown variable. We know: * New C&J = 160 kg * New Snatch = 1.80 * S * New Combined Total = New C&J + New Snatch = 250 kg Substituting the values: 160 kg + 1.80 * S = 250 kg5. **Solve the Equation:** Now we need to solve for 'S'. * Subtract 160 kg from both sides of the equation: 1.80 * S = 250 kg - 160 kg * 1.80 * S = 90 kg * Divide both sides by 1.80 to isolate S: S = 90 kg / 1.806. **Calculate the Value of S:** * S = 90 / 1.8 = 900 / 18 (multiply both numerator and denominator by 10 to remove decimal) * S = 50 (900 divided by 18 is 50. So our answer is correct.8. **State the Answer Clearly:** The initial Snatch weight was 50 kg.
