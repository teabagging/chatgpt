❓Michael ran a bakeshop and had to fill an order for 5 peach pies, 4 apple pies and 3 blueberry pies. Each pie recipe called for 3 pounds of fruit each. At the market, produce was on sale for 1.00 per pound for both blueberries and apples. The peaches each cost a certain amount per pound. 💡Let P be the number of peach pies, A be the number of apple pies and B be the number of blueberry pies.P = 5A = 4B = 3Each pie recipe calls for 3 pounds of fruit.Total pounds of fruit for peach pies = P * 3 = 5 * 3 = 15 poundsTotal pounds of fruit for apple pies = A * 3 = 4 * 3 = 12 poundsTotal pounds of fruit for blueberry pies = B * 3 = 3 * 3 = 9 poundsPrice per pound for blueberries = 1.00Price per pound for apples = 1.00Price per pound for peaches = unknown, let's call it XCost of blueberries = pounds of blueberries * price per pound of blueberries = 9 * 1.00 = 9.00Cost of apples = pounds of apples * price per pound of apples = 12 * 1.00 = 12.00Cost of peaches = pounds of peaches * price per pound of peaches = 15 * X = 15XTotal cost of fruit = Cost of blueberries + Cost of apples + Cost of peachesTotal cost of fruit = 9.00 + 12.00 + 15XMichael spent 51 at the market buying the fruit for his pie order.Total cost of fruit = 51.00So, 51.00 = 9.00 + 12.00 + 15X51.00 = 21.00 + 15XTo solve for X, we need to isolate X on one side of the equation.Subtract 21.00 from both sides of the equation:51.00 - 21.00 = 21.00 + 15X - 21.0030.00 = 15XDivide both sides by 15 to solve for X:30.00 / 15 = 15X / 152.00 = XSo, the price per pound for peaches is 2.00.Let's double check our answer.Cost of peaches = 15 pounds * 2.00/pound = 30.00Cost of apples = 12 pounds * 1.00/pound = 12.00Cost of blueberries = 9 pounds * 1.00/pound = 9.00Total cost = 30.00 + 12.00 + 9.00 = 51.00This matches the total amount Michael spent, so our answer is correct.
