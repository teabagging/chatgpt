An island is a connected component of 0s. Connectivity is defined by being adjacent in one of the four cardinal directions. A closed island is an island such that no part of it touches the boundary of the grid. In other words, every 0 that belongs to a closed island must be surrounded by 1s or other parts of the same closed island, except possibly on the borders with other parts of the same island. Thinking another way, if any part of an island is on the border of the grid (first row, last row, first column, or last column), then it cannot be a closed island. If no part of an island is on the grid boundary, then it must be a closed island.
