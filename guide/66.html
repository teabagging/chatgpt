The compound interest earned by Sunil on a certain amount at the end of two years at the rate of 10% p.a. was Rs. 420. Find the total amount that Sunil got back at the end of two years in the form of principal plus interest earned.

Let P be the principal amount.Let R be the rate of interest per annum, which is 10% or 0.10.Let T be the number of years, which is 2.The compound interest (CI) earned is given as Rs. 420.The formula for compound interest is given by:Amount (A) = P (1 + R)^TCompound Interest (CI) = A - P = P (1 + R)^T - P = P [(1 + R)^T - 1]We are given CI = 420, R = 10% = 0.10, and T = 2.We need to find the principal amount P first.420 = P [(1 + 0.10)^2 - 1]420 = P [(1.1)^2 - 1]420 = P [1.21 - 1]420 = P [0.21]Now, we need to solve for P:P = 420 / 0.21To remove the decimal in the denominator, we can multiply both numerator and denominator by 100:P = (420 * 100) / (0.21 * 100)P = 42000 / 21P = (42 * 1000) / 21P = (2 * 21 * 1000) / 21P = 2 * 1000P = 2000So, the principal amount is Rs. 2000.We need to find the total amount Sunil got back at the end of two years, which is the Amount A.Amount A = Principal P + Compound Interest CIA = P + CIA = 2000 + 420A = 2420Alternatively, we can calculate the amount A using the formula A = P (1 + R)^T:A = 2000 (1 + 0.10)^2A = 2000 (1.1)^2A = 2000 (1.21)A = 2000 * 1.21A = 2420.00A = 2420So, the total amount that Sunil got back at the end of two years is Rs. 2420.Check the CI again:CI = A - P = 2420 - 2000 = 420.This matches the given compound interest.Steps:1. Understand the given values: CI = 420, R = 10% = 0.10, T = 2.2. Use the compound interest formula: CI = P [(1 + R)^T - 1].3. Substitute the given values into the formula and solve for P.4. Calculate the total amount A = P + CI or A = P (1 + R)^T.5. Verify the answer by recalculating CI using the calculated P and given R, T.
