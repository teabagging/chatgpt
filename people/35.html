❓Point P lies on the line x= -3 and is 10 units from the point (5,2). Find the product of all possible y-coordinates that satisfy the given conditions.

💡Let P = (x,y) be a point on the line x = -3. So the x-coordinate of P is -3. So, we have the equation:sqrt{(5 - (-3))^2 + (2 - y)^2} = 10sqrt{(5 + 3)^2 + (2 - y)^2} = 10sqrt{(8)^2 + (2 - y)^2} = 10sqrt{64 + (2 - y)^2} = 10To solve for y, we square both sides of the equation:64 + (2 - y)^2 = 10^264 + (2 - y)^2 = 100Subtract 64 from both sides:(2 - y)^2 = 100 - 64(2 - y)^2 = 36Now we take the square root of both sides:2 - y = pm sqrt{36}2 - y = pm 6We have two cases:Case 1: 2 - y = 6-y = 6 - 2-y = 4y = -4So one possible y-coordinate is y = -4.Case 2: 2 - y = -6-y = -6 - 2-y = -8y = 8So another possible y-coordinate is y = 8.The possible y-coordinates are y = -4 and y = 8.We need to find the product of all possible y-coordinates.The product of the possible y-coordinates is (-4) times (8) = -32.We should check our answers.If y = -4, then P = (-3, -4). The distance between P = (-3, -4) and (5, 2) issqrt{(5 - (-3))^2 + (2 - (-4))^2} = sqrt{(5+3)^2 + (2+4)^2} = sqrt{8^2 + 6^2} = sqrt{64 + 36} = sqrt{100} = 10.So y = -4 is a valid y-coordinate.If y = 8, then P = (-3, 8).
